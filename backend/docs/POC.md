# **🧪 Proof of Concept (POC)**

**Definition:**

> A **Proof of Concept (POC)** is a **small, quick experiment or prototype** created to validate whether an **idea, approach, or technology is feasible** before committing large resources to it.

---

## **1️⃣ Why Do We Need a POC?**

* To **test feasibility** of a new idea/tech.
* To **reduce risk** before large investment.
* To **get stakeholder confidence**.
* To **uncover challenges early**.

---

## **2️⃣ Characteristics of a POC**

* **Small scope** → Not a full product.
* **Time-boxed** → Done quickly (days or weeks).
* **Focused** → Proves one specific thing.
* **Throwaway code** → Not production-ready.

---

## **3️⃣ POC vs Prototype vs MVP**

| Term          | Purpose                                     | Scope                   | Quality                         |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **POC**       | Prove feasibility of idea                   | Very limited            | Quick, experimental             |
| **Prototype** | Show **how** it works (UI/UX, flow)         | Wider than POC          | Visual, but not production code |
| **MVP**       | Deliver **minimum usable product** to users | Production-ready subset | Good enough to release          |

---

## **4️⃣ Example Scenarios**

* **Tech Validation**: Can Redis handle 1M writes/sec for our use case?
* **Integration Check**: Can our system connect with PayPal API smoothly?
* **Algorithm Feasibility**: Does this ML model detect fraud with 90% accuracy?
* **Business Idea Validation**: Will people actually use a "voice-driven grocery ordering app"?

---

## **5️⃣ POC Flow**

```
Idea → Identify Risky Assumption → Build Small Test → Validate Result → Decide (Proceed / Change / Drop)
```

---

## **6️⃣ Analogy**

> Think of a POC like **tasting a spoonful of food while cooking**:
>
> * You’re not serving the whole meal yet.
> * Just checking if the recipe works before you invest time in cooking the full dish.

---

✅ **Key takeaway:** A POC is not about delivering a product; it’s about **validating feasibility** to make informed decisions.
